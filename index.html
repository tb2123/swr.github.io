Sunspot numbers & High Speed Streams (HSS)

Major sunspot numbers resulted in coronal holes. HSS often associated with coronal holes. The solar wind speed due to coronal holes from poles is approx. 500-800km/sec while from nearby latitude ranges from 300-400km/sec. The combination of these winds is known as corotating interaction region (CIR). The combination of these winds gives compressions of slow speed winds (300-400km/s) due to high speed winds (HSS) (500-800km/s) and so we have the features like densed regions (higher density n), Larger Ap- index, negative Bz, increase in IMF (total B), moderate geomagnetic storm (-100nT <= dst<= -50nT), gradual increase in temperature and solar wind velocity tries to achieve 800km/sec but only achieves below 600km/s.
